About Stanley Mullen

Stanley H. Mullen M.D., M.A., is an emergency physician, philosopher, theologian and health-care ethicist. He has taught widely in these fields at the undergraduate and graduate levels, and has written and lectured widely on these topics, including a lenghty book titled The Problem of Suffering. He is both a theist and a recovering agnostic. Dr. Mullen and his wife, artist Sally Elliott, live in the mountains north of Boulder, Colorado.

Philosophy of Religion

Dr. Stanley H. Mullen addresses some of the perennial issues in Philosophy of Religion -- the problem of suffering, the coherence of the idea of God, the dialogue between science and religion, the interface of morality and cultural influence, as well as such ideas as the Tao, Sehnsucht, the mystical, the numinous, religous experience, and the rise of the New Atheism. New Swways in Philosophy of Religion is a hospitable, yet challenging, exploration of timeless human question for both the novice and the academic reader.
